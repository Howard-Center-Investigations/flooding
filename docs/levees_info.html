Levees


The map shows two levee systems: Anacostia and Washington D.C.

District of Columbia Levee System (DCLS)

The D.C. levee is designed to hold back major Potomac River floods. It consists of approximately 0.75 miles of levee along the north side of the reflecting pool, 0.05 miles of floodwall (wing walls of the 17th St. closure structure), and 3 closure structures. There are two sandbag closures located at 23rd St. and Constitution Ave., and at P and Canal Streets. There is also a newly constructed (2013) post and panel closure structure at 17th Street.

Anacostia system

The Anacostia system consists of approximately 11,600’ of levee, 1,200’ of floodwall, 328’ seaplane closure which has been closed permanently, two small closure structures which provide access to boat piers, and four pump stations.

The levee data was obtained through National Levee Database
